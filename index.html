Company at a Glance

Knowledge Marine & Engineering Works Ltd (KMEW), incorporated in 2015, is India's fastest-growing marine infrastructure services company. Based in Mumbai, KMEW specialises in dredging operations, owning and chartering marine crafts, repair and maintenance of vessels, and — since Q3 FY26 — commercial shipbuilding. The company serves government clients (IWAI, Deendayal Port) and private port operators.

KMEW's fleet includes pilot boats, speed patrol boats, survey boats, grab dredgers, trailing suction hopper dredgers (TSHDs), mooring launches, and service boats. Its operational marine assets include River Pearl 1 through River Pearl 12 and growing. The company operates in India and Myanmar.

In Q3 FY26, KMEW elected the tonnage tax scheme — a transformational financial decision that reduces effective tax rate to below 1%, dramatically improving net profitability even without revenue growth. Combined with the entry into commercial shipbuilding and a ₹1,500 Cr order book, the growth outlook is compelling.

Competitive Moat Analysis
Operational Excellence vs DCI (State-Owned Competitor)
DCI (Dredging Corporation of India) is India's largest dredger but KMEW consistently outperforms it on margins (40%+ vs DCI's ~20%) and growth. KMEW's agility and project discipline are its core competitive advantages.
Younger Fleet = Lower Maintenance, Better Bids
Modern, well-maintained fleet enables KMEW to bid competitively on quality-sensitive government contracts where older vessels don't qualify. Younger fleet also reduces downtime and operating cost.
Government Client Relationships (IWAI, Ports)
Long-standing relationships with IWAI (Inland Waterways Authority of India) and major port trusts. Repeat business from existing clients — 50%+ bid win rate demonstrates trust and execution track record.
Tonnage Tax — Structural Profitability Advantage
New tonnage tax election reduces effective tax rate from 20-25% to below 1%. This is a permanent structural advantage under Indian shipping tax law. No competitor that hasn't made this election can match KMEW's net margin.
Shipbuilding Entry — New Vertical With Captive Demand
KMEW can build vessels for its own fleet (reducing vessel acquisition cost) and for external clients (new revenue). Vertical integration of fleet ownership + construction is rare among Indian marine companies.
EBITDA Margin Discipline (35%+ Target)
Management explicitly targets minimum 35% EBITDA margin on all bids. Selective bidding on premium contracts — refuses low-margin work that DCI might accept.

Growth Triggers
HIGHTonnage Tax — Structural PAT Multiplier
The tonnage tax election reduces KMEW's effective tax rate from ~20% to below 1%. This is not a one-time event — it is a permanent structural change under Indian shipping tax law (Section 115V of the Income Tax Act). Every future rupee of EBITDA now converts to PAT at 98%+ efficiency. For FY27, assuming ₹130-140 Cr EBITDA, PAT could reach ₹120-135 Cr vs only ₹105 Cr that would have been achievable at 25% tax rate.
▲ Structural PAT uplift of 25%+ vs pre-tonnage-tax scenario — permanent.
▼ Any change in tonnage tax scheme applicability by government would reverse this benefit.
HIGH₹700 Cr Green Tug Contract (15-Year Revenue Lock-In)
A 15-year green tug contract is categorically different from KMEW's typical project contracts. It guarantees ₹40-50 Cr annual revenue for 15 years from a single contract — effectively ₹700 Cr total lifetime contract value with high visibility. Green tug contracts globally carry premium margins as operators pay up for regulatory compliance. This single contract transforms KMEW's earnings quality.
▲ ₹40-50 Cr guaranteed annual revenue for 15 years from one contract alone — high-margin, low-execution-risk.
▼ Contract details and counterparty creditworthiness not fully disclosed — verify before building full base case on this.
HIGHShipbuilding — ₹500-700 Cr Revenue Target in 3 Years
Entry into commercial shipbuilding via ₹230 Cr IWAI orders is just the opening position. Management is investing ₹100 Cr in shipyard infrastructure to target ₹500-700 Cr shipbuilding revenue within 3 years. IWAI's massive fleet replacement programme (105+ new vessels planned) is the captive demand base. KMEW can also build for its own fleet at lower-than-market cost.
▲ ₹500 Cr shipbuilding + ₹300 Cr dredging = ₹800 Cr potential revenue by FY29 on this trajectory.
▼ Shipbuilding is technically complex; scaling execution without quality issues is a real risk for a company entering a new vertical.
MEDIUMOrder Book Execution — ₹1,500 Cr Backlog + ₹3,000 Cr Pipeline
With ₹1,500 Cr order book and ₹3,000+ Cr bid pipeline as of Q3 FY26, near-term revenue visibility is strong. The key question is execution pace — historically KMEW has executed well, with Q3 FY26 showing ₹90 Cr quarterly revenue as first evidence of order book monetisation acceleration.
▲ ₹1,500 Cr order book implies ~5-6 quarters of revenue at current run-rate — strong visibility.
▼ Top 5 orders = 74% of book — concentration risk. Any one large order delay materially impacts quarterly numbers.
OPTIONALITYBahrain Project & International Expansion
KMEW secured Bahrain sand mining contract (operational pause currently) and is exploring cruise vessel operations. International diversification would reduce India government client concentration. Myanmar operations also provide geographic spread.
▲ International revenue at higher margins — pure upside if Bahrain resumes.
▼ International projects carry execution, regulatory, and geopolitical complexity. Not in base case.

Investment Thesis

Why KMEW? KMEW is the rare combination of structural sector tailwind (India's waterway and port infrastructure buildout) + operational excellence (40%+ EBITDA margins vs industry average of 20%) + financial engineering (tonnage tax turning effective tax rate to near-zero) all arriving simultaneously. The company has compounded revenue at 53% CAGR for 5 years — one of the highest in India's listed infrastructure universe — and is now entering its next growth phase via commercial shipbuilding.

Why Now? Three catalysts are converging in FY26-27: (1) Tonnage tax election that permanently improves net margins; (2) ₹700 Cr green tug contract providing 15-year revenue certainty; and (3) entry into commercial shipbuilding just as IWAI launches a massive vessel procurement programme. The Q3 FY26 quarter (₹90 Cr revenue, ₹33 Cr PAT, PAT margin 37%!) is the first data point showing this convergence in financial results. FII entry to 11% in December 2025 confirms institutional recognition of the same thesis.

What Is the Market Missing? The market is focused on the historical PE of 61x and questioning the valuation. What it is not fully pricing is the forward PAT trajectory with tonnage tax. At 1% effective tax rate on ₹140 Cr FY27E EBITDA, PAT could be ₹120-130 Cr — implying the stock is trading at only 32-34x forward earnings. For a company growing revenue at 40-50% with improving margins and a locked 15-year revenue contract, this is attractive.

The constraint: This is a small-cap with 2.37 Cr shares outstanding — very limited liquidity. The 53.6% promoter stake provides reasonable comfort, but the continuous dilution trend (from 67% to 53%) warrants monitoring. Government client concentration (80%+ from government entities) means any policy slowdown hits immediately.
